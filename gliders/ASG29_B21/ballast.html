Flying with Ballast

Do not attempt to land the glider while carrying ballast.

You and the glider will probably survive the landing but the stresses on the wings are likely exceed the limits of the aircraft.

For a long time, high-performance gliders have benefitted from an ability to carry additional weight which can be dumped if the soaring conditions become weak. Aviation regulations permit pilots to eject three things out of aircraft:

  1. Water
  2. Sand
  3. People

For various practical reasons, water is the preferred modern choice for this dumpable ballast and the ASG29 can carry 200 liters (i.e. Kg) of water, adding approximately 50% to the all-up weight of the aircraft to a max of 600Kg. We usually refer to the ,a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wing_loading">wing loading of an aircraft (i.e. weight / wing area) rather than simply the weight as this value is then comparable between different aircraft, with an ASK21 maximum wing loading of 33Kg/m^2 being less than the ASG29 minimum, and the fully-ballasted maximum for the ASG29 is 57Kg/m^2 which is exceptionally high.

The use of ballast relies upon one particular and unexpected aspect of glider aerodynamics:

When weight is added to a glider, the aircraft will sink faster and also glide faster, and in general sink speed and airspeed will alter in the same ratio.

This means that the best glide ratio is effectively the same at different levels of ballast but the heavier the glider, the faster speed at which best glide is achieved. The effect is illustrated in the ASG29 polar diagram (of airspeed vs. sink) for the min and max wing-loadings with best glide at 100km/h and 125km/h respectively (54 knots and 67 knots).

The rest of this guide will refer to the ASG29 ballast as 0%..100%, as this is how it is displayed in the on-panel indicator. 100% equates to 200 liters / 200Kg of water ballast.

Filling and dumping ballast in the ASG29

The ballast indicator is on the lower right of the panel:

Filling the ballast

On the ground, the ASG29_B21 will default to starting with 25% ballast. The glider can then be filled with ballast by clicking the     icon, at which point the indicator will read 100% as above. You cannot fill the ballast while in the air...

Dumping the ballast

The OPEN and CLOSE buttons on the ballast indicator control the dump valve.

From full, the ballast takes 4 minutes to dump. This means on a fast straight glide to the airfield you will begin dumping 15km / 9 miles away. (At 150kph / 80 knots the distance would be 10km / 6 miles).

To dump 50% of the ballast, you OPEN the dump valve (for 2 minutes) and then CLOSE it again.

Launching

Winch

It would be relatively unusual to winch launch the ASG29 at all as it is an expensive aircraft, and even more unlikely to be carry water ballast during a winch launch. The issue is the relatively high risk of a launch failure when the ballast would make the ASG29 more difficult to handle and have zero chance of dumping the ballast before landing.

The X-Plane default of 25% ballast is probably the maximum reasonable for a winch launch in the ASG29.
